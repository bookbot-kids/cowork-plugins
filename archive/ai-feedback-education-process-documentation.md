**2. Kestin et al., 2025 (Harvard University)**
- **Citation:** Kestin, G., Miller, K., Klales, A., Milbourne, T., & Ponti, G. (2025). AI tutoring outperforms in-class active learning: an RCT introducing a novel research-based design in an authentic educational setting. *Scientific Reports*, 15, 17458.
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12179260/
- **Sample size:** n=194 students (Harvard Physics 2 course)
- **Key findings:**
  - AI group median post-test: 4.5 vs in-class: 3.5
  - Learning gains over double for AI group (p < 10⁻⁸)
  - Effect size: 0.63-1.3 standard deviations
  - Time: 49 min vs 60 min
  - Engagement: 4.1 vs 3.6 (5-point scale)

**3. Zhai et al., 2024**
- **Citation:** Zhai, C., Wibowo, S., & Li, L. D. (2024). The effects of over-reliance on AI dialogue systems on students' cognitive abilities: A systematic review. *Smart Learning Environments*, 11(1).
- **URL:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/381518778
- **Sample size:** Systematic review
- **Key finding:** Over-reliance on AI dialogue systems impairs decision-making, critical thinking, and analytical reasoning; leads to diminished creativity and reduced critical thinking skills
